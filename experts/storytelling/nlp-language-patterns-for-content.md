# NLP Language Patterns for Content Marketing

## Source
Extracted and adapted from: Ross Jeffries "Secrets of Speed Seduction" + IN10SE "The October Man Sequence". All seduction context removed. Patterns repurposed for LinkedIn posts, video scripts, and carousels that create emotional engagement and convert followers into clients.

---

## Core Principle: Everything is Process

The foundational insight: emotions like trust, excitement, curiosity, urgency, and desire to buy are NOT random events. They are **internal processes** that can be activated through language.

**The key shift**: stop thinking "how do I sell my product?" → start thinking "what STATES do I want my reader to experience, and in what ORDER?"

### The State Sequence for Content That Converts

Every high-converting piece of content follows this sequence:

```
1. CAPTURE ATTENTION → Hook (curiosity, shock, pattern interrupt)
2. BUILD CONNECTION → "I understand your world" (empathy, recognition)
3. AMPLIFY DESIRE → Create/intensify the pain or desire state
4. LINK TO SOLUTION → Your brand/product becomes the natural answer
```

**Rule**: Always go from least intrusive to most intrusive. Don't pitch in the hook. Build connection FIRST.

## Pattern 1: Embedded Commands

### What They Are
Commands hidden inside normal sentences that the reader's unconscious mind processes and follows, without triggering resistance.

### The Formula
**Weasel Phrase** + **Command Verb** + **Desired State** = Embedded Command

### Weasel Phrases (Content Marketing Version)

| Weasel Phrase | Why It Works | Content Example |
|---|---|---|
| **When you...** | Presupposes the reader WILL do/feel it | "When you **start seeing results**, the whole game changes." |
| **What would it be like if...** | Commands them to imagine it | "What would it be like if you could **generate leads on autopilot**?" |
| **A person can...** | Deflects resistance — you're talking about "a person" | "A person can **feel completely confident** once they have the right framework." |
| **If you were to...** | Hypothetical = no resistance | "If you were to **simplify your workflow**, what would that free up?" |
| **As you...** | Assumes they're already doing it | "As you **read this carefully**, you'll notice something shifting." |
| **It's not necessary to...** | Negation: says "don't" but the mind DOES | "It's not necessary to **decide right now** — just notice how this feels." |
| **You might find...** | Implies it will just happen naturally | "You might find that **ideas start flowing** as soon as you try this." |
| **How surprised would you be to...** | Presupposes the event WILL happen | "How surprised would you be to **double your engagement** this month?" |
| **Invite you to notice...** | Gentle, voluntary, hard to resist | "I invite you to notice how **your perspective is already shifting**." |

### Command Verbs (For Marketing Content)

| Verb | Use |
|---|---|
| **Imagine** | Visualization, desire-building |
| **Notice** | Attention direction |
| **Remember** | Past experience activation |
| **Experience** | State creation |
| **Discover** | Curiosity amplification |
| **Realize** | Belief shift |
| **Feel** | Emotional engagement |
| **Decide** | Action commitment |
| **Start** | Behavior initiation |

### Practical Application: LinkedIn Post

```
❌ WEAK: "Our tool helps you save time."

✅ WITH EMBEDDED COMMANDS:
"When you start to IMAGINE what your morning would feel like
without the chaos of manual reporting...

You might find that what changes isn't the tool.
It's the WAY YOU START TO THINK about your time.

As you NOTICE that shift happening,
you can DECIDE that this is the week it changes."
```

## Pattern 2: "Have You Ever" — The State Activation Question

### Why It's Devastating
"Have you ever" SEEMS like a question but it's actually a COMMAND. It forces the reader to go inside their memory, find a matching experience, and RE-LIVE the emotional state.

### The Formula

1. Ask "Have you ever [named state]?"
2. Describe what that state feels like (sensory details)
3. Amplify the state with embedded commands
4. Link the state to your solution / brand

### Marketing Applications

| Goal | "Have You Ever" Opening |
|---|---|
| **Build empathy** | "Have you ever stayed up until 2 AM trying to figure out why your campaigns aren't working?" |
| **Create desire** | "Have you ever had that moment where everything just CLICKS and you know exactly what to do next?" |
| **Activate frustration** | "Have you ever watched a competitor with a worse product get 10x your traction?" |
| **Inspire possibility** | "Have you ever met someone and IMMEDIATELY known they were going to change your life?" |

### Extended Example (LinkedIn Post)

```
Have you ever opened your analytics dashboard
and felt that sinking feeling?

You know the one.

Where the numbers don't lie,
but you wish they would.

The campaigns you poured energy into...
barely a blip.

Meanwhile, that competitor
(the one with the ugly website)
is everywhere.

Here's what I discovered after 3 years
of feeling exactly that way:

The problem was never the content.
It was the FIRST 3 SECONDS.

[Rest of post with framework]
```

## Pattern 3: The Power of Negation

### Mechanism
When you say "DON'T think about X", the mind MUST think about X to process the sentence. Negation becomes an invisible command.

### Content Marketing Negation Formulas

| Formula | Example |
|---|---|
| **"Don't [desired action] yet"** | "Don't start optimizing your funnel yet — first read this." |
| **"You don't have to [desired state]"** | "You don't have to feel excited about these results, but..." |
| **"It's not necessary to [desired action]"** | "It's not necessary to change your strategy immediately." |
| **"I wouldn't want you to [desired thought]"** | "I wouldn't want you to imagine what 3x revenue would mean for your team." |
| **"You really shouldn't [desired action]"** | "You really shouldn't share this with your competitors." |
| **"This isn't about [desired state]"** | "This isn't about making you feel urgency. It's about showing you data." |

### LinkedIn Application

```
I'm NOT going to tell you that your current
LinkedIn strategy is wasting your time.

That's not for me to say.

But I will share 5 data points
that changed the way I think about this platform.

You don't have to change anything.

Just... notice if any of these surprise you.
```

*Why this works: every negation is actually a command. "NOT wasting your time" = reader thinks about wasting time. "Don't have to change" = reader considers changing.*

## Pattern 4: Quotes Pattern — Saying the Unsayable

### Mechanism
Instead of making a bold claim directly (which triggers resistance), you QUOTE someone else saying it. The reader processes the content without defending against it.

### Content Marketing Quotes Formulas

| Formula | Example |
|---|---|
| **"My client told me..."** | "My client told me: 'This changed everything. I wish I'd found it 2 years ago.'" |
| **"I read something that..."** | "I read something that blew my mind: 'The best marketing feels like a conversation, not a pitch.'" |
| **"Someone once said..."** | "Someone once said: 'The cost of ignoring LinkedIn in 2026 isn't $0. It's everything you're leaving on the table.'" |
| **"A mentor told me..."** | "A mentor told me: 'Stop creating content. Start creating experiences.'" |
| **"The data says..."** | "The data says something uncomfortable: 97% of your content is invisible." |

### Extended LinkedIn Example

```
A founder I work with looked me dead in the eye
and said something I'll never forget:

"I spent €200K on ads last year.
You know what outperformed all of it?

12 LinkedIn posts.

Twelve.

Not viral ones. Not clever ones.
Just posts where I talked like a human
about problems my customers actually have."

That conversation changed how I think
about content forever.

Here's the framework behind those 12 posts:
[...]
```

## Pattern 5: Describing Another Person's Experience

### Mechanism
Instead of telling the reader "YOU will feel X" (which triggers resistance), you describe a THIRD PERSON experiencing X. The reader's mind processes it as if it's happening to them — without resistance.

### Content Marketing Application

```
❌ DIRECT: "You'll feel confident when you use our framework."

✅ THIRD PERSON EXPERIENCE:
"When Sara implemented this framework in her first week,
something shifted. She described it as this moment
where the noise just... stopped.

She could see exactly what to post,
when to post, what to say.

Not because she suddenly became creative.
But because the structure freed her up
to actually think."
```

### Why It's Powerful for Case Studies
Every case study should be written as a third-person experience pattern. Don't list features. DESCRIBE the transformation as a lived experience.

## Pattern 6: Time Distortion

### Mechanism
Compress or expand time to create emotional effect. Make the reader feel like they've already experienced a result, or transport them into a future where they've already succeeded.

### Time Distortion Formulas for Content

| Type | Formula | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Future memory** | "Imagine looking back 6 months from now..." | "Imagine looking back on this moment as the one where everything changed." |
| **Compressed past** | "In just [short time], [big result]" | "In 48 hours, our client went from 0 leads to a full pipeline." |
| **Already happened** | "When you've already [result]..." | "When you've already built your audience, you'll see why this step mattered most." |
| **Retrospective** | "You'll look back on today as..." | "You'll look back on reading this as the moment it clicked." |

### LinkedIn Post Structure Using Time Distortion

```
6 months ago, I was staring at 47 followers.

Today, I'm writing this post
to [large number] of you.

And the thing that changed wasn't the algorithm.
It wasn't a viral post.

It was ONE decision I made on a random Tuesday.

Here's what I decided:
[...]

6 months from now, you could be writing
a post exactly like this one.

The question isn't IF it'll work.
It's when you'll start.
```

## Pattern 7: The Fisherman Metaphor (Ethical Persuasion)

### Principle
Never "pull the fishing line" hard. The reader shouldn't feel pushed. Instead, you MOVE YOURSELF CLOSER to them. You reduce resistance by making each step feel natural.

### Content Application Rules

1. **Never pitch in the hook** — capture attention first
2. **Build connection before asking for anything** — empathy, shared experience
3. **Make the CTA feel like THEIR idea** — "If this resonated, you might want to..."
4. **Use gradual escalation** — free content → value → trust → offer
5. **Soft language for hard intentions** — "I'm curious if..." instead of "Buy now"

### Softener Phrases for Content CTAs

| Softener | When to Use |
|---|---|
| "I'm curious if..." | Before asking a question |
| "If this resonated..." | Before suggesting an action |
| "You might find it interesting that..." | Before sharing something self-promotional |
| "Just for the sake of understanding better..." | Before asking for personal info |
| "I don't know if this applies to you, but..." | Before a targeted offer |

## Pattern 8: Anchoring — Making Feelings Stick

### Content Marketing Anchoring
In written content you can't use touch, but you CAN create anchoring through:

| Anchor Type | How It Works | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Recurring phrase** | A signature phrase repeated across posts | "That's the real game." (used consistently) |
| **Visual anchor** | Specific emoji or formatting pattern linked to a state | 🔥 always before the key insight |
| **Structural anchor** | Same post format = reader immediately knows the "vibe" | Every Monday post = tactical, every Friday = storytelling |
| **Sound anchor** | For video/audio — specific music, tone, sound cue | A specific intro sound = "time for value" |

### How to Build Brand Anchoring on LinkedIn

1. **Choose 2-3 signature phrases** that encapsulate your brand's core feelings
2. **Use them consistently** across all content
3. **Link them to specific emotional states** — e.g., "This is the shift." = moment of realization
4. Over time, the reader will feel the state JUST from reading the phrase

## Pattern 9: Sub-Modalities — Making Mental Images More Powerful

### Principle
People's mental images have STRUCTURE: size, brightness, position, movement. By directing HOW the reader visualizes, you control the emotional intensity.

### Writing Techniques for Manipulating Mental Images

| Technique | How to Write It | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Make it bigger** | "Picture that pipeline growing... wider, bigger..." | Amplifies positive emotion |
| **Make it brighter** | "Imagine that idea shining brighter and clearer..." | Increases certainty |
| **Add movement** | "See yourself walking into that meeting, confident..." | Makes it feel real |
| **Associate (1st person)** | "See it through YOUR eyes, feel the keyboard under your fingers..." | Maximum emotional impact |
| **Dissociate (3rd person)** | "Step back and see yourself in this situation..." | Creates distance, useful for pain points |

### LinkedIn Example — Sub-modality Escalation

```
Close your eyes for a second.
(I know, weird thing to ask on LinkedIn. Bear with me.)

Picture your workspace Monday morning.

Now picture it with ZERO unread messages.
No urgent Slack pings.
No "can we hop on a quick call?"

Just... a clean slate.
Your priority list, clear as glass.
Your calendar, breathing room.

Make that picture bigger.
Brighter.

That's not a fantasy.
That's what happens when you [framework/tool].
```

## Pattern 10: Values Elicitation — The Deep Connection Pattern (Full 3-Phase System)

### Mechanism
Instead of guessing what your audience wants, systematically ELICIT their deepest values, RANK them, and then TRIGGER an intense connection by naming those values back in the exact right order.

### Phase 1: Elicit Values (Content Research)

**Ask**: "What's important to you in [topic/area]?"
→ Surface answers: "results", "quality", "speed", "growth"

**Go deeper**: "What does having [surface answer] allow you to EXPERIENCE that you wouldn't otherwise experience?"
→ Deeper values: "freedom", "respect", "peace of mind", "control", "trust"

**Keep digging**: "What else is important to you?"
→ Repeat until you have 3-5 core values

### Phase 2: Rank Values (Priority Mapping)

**Ask**: "Of those values, which is the MOST important — the one you absolutely MUST have?"

Then rank the rest. This gives you a **Values Hierarchy** — the exact order in which to sequence your content messaging.

| Audience Segment | Top Value | Second Value | Third Value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Solopreneurs | Freedom | Control | Growth |
| Agency owners | Scalability | Trust | Profit |
| Corporate leaders | Authority | Results | Legacy |

### Phase 3: Trigger Connection (The Values Cascade)

When you name ALL of someone's values, IN ORDER of importance, it creates a profound emotional state — a feeling of being deeply understood.

**The Formula**: Name values 1 → 2 → 3 in a single statement or closing paragraph.

```
❌ GENERIC: "Our tool saves you time."

✅ FULL VALUES CASCADE:
"I've been thinking about what we're really building here.

And I think it comes down to three things:

Freedom — to choose HOW you work.
Control — over what gets your attention.
And growth — that doesn't cost you your sanity.

If you felt something reading that...
that's how you know these are YOUR values.

Everything I create is built around these three things."
```

**Why this works**: When someone's values are named back to them in the right order, they enter a state of deep recognition — "this person GETS me." This is one of the most powerful connection techniques in all of NLP.

### In Audience Research Posts (LinkedIn)
Ask your audience directly:
- "When it comes to [topic], what matters MOST to you?"
- "If you could fix ONE thing about [process], what would it be?"
- "What does [desired outcome] actually MEAN to you, personally?"

## Pattern 11: The Generalized Super-Pattern — The Universal 4-Step Formula

### The Master Template
Every advanced influence pattern in both ebooks follows the SAME 4-step structure. This is the meta-pattern behind all the individual patterns:

| Step | Action | Content Marketing Application |
|---|---|---|
| **1. "Have you ever...?"** | Access a past experience / state | Open with a relatable memory or situation |
| **2. Tell a story** | Describe someone else experiencing the desired state | Use case study, client story, or third-person experience |
| **3. Describe the process** | Unpack the experience with embedded commands (link to you if positive, to competitor if negative) | Walk the reader through the transformation step by step |
| **4. Move the mental picture** | Shift where/how the reader sees the concept in their mind (sub-modality shift) | Reframe the reader's entire perspective with a paradigm-shifting insight |

### LinkedIn Post Using the Full Super-Pattern

```
Have you ever scrolled through LinkedIn
and felt like everyone has it figured out except you?

I had a client, Maria, who felt exactly that.
She'd sit down to write a post and just... freeze.
Every word felt forced. Every idea felt stolen.

But here's what happened when she discovered
her content isn't about HER — it's about the PROCESS
her reader goes through while reading.

She stopped asking "What should I post?"
and started asking "What should they FEEL?"

Suddenly the words came easy.
The engagement followed.

Now take that picture of content creation
you've had in your head — the hard one.
The one that feels like pulling teeth.

Put it aside.

And put THIS picture there instead:
You, writing in 15-minute bursts.
Focused. Clear. Enjoying it.

That's not a fantasy.
That's a framework away.
```

### Why This Matters
This 4-step formula works because it follows the brain's natural processing sequence: access memory → empathize → experience → reframe. Use it as a **universal template** whenever you're stuck on post structure.

## Pattern 12: Tonality in Written Content

### Principle
In spoken persuasion, TONALITY carries 50% of the persuasive power — a downward shift in voice makes a statement land as a command rather than a suggestion. In written content, you simulate tonality through **formatting, punctuation, sentence rhythm, and structural pacing**.

### Written Tonality Techniques

| Spoken Tonality | Written Equivalent | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Downward shift** (command) | Short. Standalone. Sentence. | "That's the real game." |
| **Upward shift** (question/curiosity) | "...right?" or "...isn't it?" | "Interesting, isn't it?" |
| **Whisper** (intimate, confessional) | Parenthetical or em-dash aside | "(Here's the part nobody tells you.)" |
| **Pause** (dramatic effect) | White space + one-word line | [...line break...] "Exactly." |
| **Volume increase** (emphasis) | CAPS on key words (sparingly) | "This is what ACTUALLY works." |
| **Warmth/softness** | Longer, flowing sentences with sensory language | "And slowly, like fog lifting off a lake, everything starts to become clear..." |
| **Speed increase** (urgency) | Stacked short sentences, no conjunctions | "Post it. Share it. Watch it work." |

### The Tonal Shift Pattern for LinkedIn Posts

The most engaging posts alternate between "speeds": slow/warm storytelling → fast/punchy insights → slow/reflective close.

```
[WARM — slow, sensory]
There's this moment every morning.
Before the inbox. Before Slack.
Where it's just you and the work.

[FAST — punchy command]
Protect that moment.
Guard it.
It's where the real thinking happens.

[WARM — reflective close]
Because the best ideas don't come
from responding faster.
They come from thinking slower.
```

## Pattern 13: The Confusion Pattern — Objection Neutralizer

### Mechanism
When someone's objection is met with a response so layered and complex that it becomes hard to follow, the original objection gets "lost" — the critical factor can't hold onto it. The mind moves past the objection and opens up to new input.

### Content Application
Use this when addressing common objections in content. Don't dismiss the objection — make it DISSOLVE by layering perspective on top of perspective.

### The Formula
1. **Acknowledge the objection** — validate it's understandable
2. **Layer 3+ perspectives** — what you know, what you know you know, what you don't know you know
3. **Land a reframe** — the new perspective replaces the objection

### LinkedIn Example — Dissolving "I Don't Have Time for Content"

```
"I don't have time to post on LinkedIn."

I hear this every week.

And I get it. I really do.

But here's the thing about time:
There's the time you think you have.
There's the time you actually have.
And there's the time you'd have
if you stopped spending 3 hours a day
on things that won't matter in 6 months.

So when someone says "I don't have time" —
what they really mean is
"I haven't yet realized how much time
I'm already losing by NOT doing this."

Once you see it that way,
the question isn't about finding time.

It's about choosing what to spend it on.

And that's a very different conversation.
```

**Why this works**: By the time the reader gets through the layers, the original "I don't have time" objection has been replaced by a new frame — "I'm losing time by NOT doing this." The objection didn't get argued against; it got dissolved.

## Quick Reference: Pattern Cheat Sheet

| Pattern | Use In Content When... | Key Phrase |
|---|---|---|
| **Embedded Commands** | You want subtle persuasion | "When you...", "As you...", "You might find..." |
| **Have You Ever** | You want to activate a past emotional state | "Have you ever..." |
| **Negation** | You want to plant an idea without resistance | "Don't...", "It's not necessary to..." |
| **Quotes** | You want to say something bold indirectly | "My client said...", "I read that..." |
| **Third Person** | You want to describe transformation without resistance | "When Sara started using this..." |
| **Time Distortion** | You want to create urgency or possibility | "6 months from now...", "Looking back on today..." |
| **Fisherman/Softeners** | You want a non-aggressive CTA | "If this resonated...", "I'm curious if..." |
| **Anchoring** | You want recurring emotional associations | Signature phrases, consistent formats |
| **Sub-Modalities** | You want vivid visualization | "Make the picture bigger/brighter..." |
| **Values Elicitation** | You want deep emotional targeting + the values cascade | "What does [X] actually mean to you?" → Name all 3 values |
| **Super-Pattern** | You need a universal post template | Have you ever → Story → Process → Reframe |
| **Tonality** | You want to control pacing and emotional weight | Short. Lines. = commands / flowing prose = warmth |
| **Confusion Pattern** | You need to dissolve an objection | Layer perspectives until objection dissolves |

## Frameworks and Methodologies

### The C.O.R.E. System (Creator Operating System)
A proprietary framework from the Awesome Creator Academy:

#### C — Content Strategy
- Define your content pillars
- Create a sustainable editorial calendar
- Identify the formats that work for your niche
- Balance evergreen and trending content

#### O — Optimization and Systems
- Optimize titles, thumbnails, descriptions for YouTube SEO
- Create repeatable production systems (batch recording, templates, checklists)
- Automate where possible (scheduling, repurposing, analytics)
- Build a workflow that doesn't depend on motivation

#### R — Revenue Generation
- Don't depend on a single revenue source
- Diversify: YouTube Partner Program, brand deals, UGC, affiliate marketing, digital products, coaching
- Build a "revenue stack" — each layer supports the next
- Free content is the marketing for paid products

#### E — Engagement and Audience Growth
- Reply to comments — builds community and signals to the algorithm
- Collaborate with other creators in your niche
- Create content that generates conversation, not just views
- Build a community, not just an audience

### "Create Value First" Framework
The creation hierarchy:
1. **Value for the Viewer**: what they learn, what they feel, what they can do after watching
2. **Audience Growth**: value creates loyalty, loyalty creates organic growth
3. **Monetization**: only after building value and audience, monetize
4. **Scaling**: once the model is validated, scale with team and systems

### Revenue Diversification Model
Roberto practices and teaches:
- **YouTube Partner Program**: the base, but never the only source
- **Brand Deals and UGC**: collaborations with brands aligned to your values
- **Affiliate Marketing**: recommending products you actually use
- **Digital Products**: courses, templates, ebooks
- **Coaching and Consulting**: monetizing expertise directly
- **Speaking**: paid keynotes and workshops

## How to Apply to Your Brand

### The C.O.R.E. System for Business Channels
Apply the C.O.R.E. framework to [your brand]'s YouTube channel:
- **Content Strategy**: define 3-5 thematic pillars aligned with your target's pain points
- **Optimization**: create thumbnail templates, publication checklists, repeatable production workflows
- **Revenue**: even if the channel doesn't monetize directly, every video must generate measurable value (leads, brand awareness, customer education)
- **Engagement**: reply to every comment, create videos based on audience questions, feature clients as protagonists

### Consistency > Perfection
Don't wait for the perfect setup to get started. Publish with what you have, improve along the way. HubSpot started its YouTube channel with simple videos — today it has millions of views. The first video doesn't need to be perfect, it needs to exist.

### Revenue Stack for Brands
Even a brand can build a "revenue stack" from its YouTube channel:
- **Layer 1**: awareness and brand building (views, reach)
- **Layer 2**: lead generation (in-video CTA to landing page)
- **Layer 3**: customer education (tutorials that reduce churn)
- **Layer 4**: community (comments, lives, membership)
- **Layer 5**: partnerships and co-marketing (collaborations with other brands/creators)

### Value Above All
Every video from [your brand] must pass the "value test": did the viewer learn something new? Can they do something they couldn't do before? Did they change their perspective on a topic? If the answer is no, the video isn't ready. Example: Notion doesn't publish a video unless it teaches something specific and actionable.

### Content That Lasts 5 Years
Ask yourself for every video: "Will this still be relevant in 5 years?" If yes, it's evergreen content — the type of content with the highest ROI on YouTube. If not, it must be timely and relevant enough to justify its short shelf life.

# MrBeast (Jimmy Donaldson) — YouTube Expert Profile

## Profile
- **Who he is**: Jimmy Donaldson, known as MrBeast, is the biggest creator in YouTube history. He started uploading videos in 2012 at age 13, spending years studying the algorithm and virality. He built a media empire (Beast Industries) that includes Feastables (snacks), Beast Burger, and the fintech app Step. Forbes has valued him at over $1 billion. In 2026 he is evolving toward more narrative and emotional content, with character arcs and longer formats.
- **YouTube subscribers**: ~467 million (main channel), nearly 1 billion total followers across all platforms
- **Total views**: over 50 billion views
- **Specialization**: extreme-scale production, retention, thumbnails, packaging, total reinvestment of revenue into content
- **Channel**: MrBeast

## Core Philosophy

1. **Total reinvestment**: "I've reinvested everything to the point of stupidity." 95-98% of earnings are reinvested in production. Higher budgets = more impressive productions = larger audience = higher revenue = even bigger budgets. A virtuous cycle.
2. **Quality > quantity**: a single video with 5 million views is more valuable than 100 videos with 50,000 views each. Better to invest in a few excellent videos than to produce many mediocre ones.
3. **The best YOUTUBE video, not the best video period**: "Your goal is to make the best YOUTUBE videos possible. Not the best produced videos... the best YOUTUBE videos." Production serves YouTube, not the other way around.
4. **Obsession with the first seconds**: the first 3-5 seconds decide everything. Always open with an extreme action or a statement that creates immediate tension.
5. **Continuous evolution**: in 2026 MrBeast acknowledged that audiences have become "numb" to spectacles. The new frontier is emotional depth, narrative arcs, and character development.

## Frameworks and Methodologies

### Thumbnail Strategy
- **High-contrast colors**: bright yellows, intense reds, vivid blues that stand out against YouTube's white interface
- **The 0-2 word rule**: thumbnails with the most views have 0 or at most 2 words
- **One face, strong emotion**: one person as the primary focus with a happy/excited expression
- **The thumbnail determines success**: it can mean tens of millions of views difference. MrBeast tests thumbnails before even filming the video

### Retention Editing
- High-impact sound effects, rapid cuts, zero pauses
- Every second must justify its existence in the video
- Constant pattern interrupts to maintain attention
- Narrative structure with rising tension

### Global Scalability
- Videos available in 40+ languages
- Professional dubbing and subtitles for every market
- Content designed to be universally understandable

### Iterative Production
- Every video is analyzed in minute detail post-publication
- Continuous A/B testing on thumbnails and titles
- Dedicated team for retention curve analysis

## Hook and Retention Techniques

- **Extreme hook**: open with the most spectacular action or the strongest promise of the video
- **Constant escalation**: every segment must surpass the previous one in intensity
- **Eliminate "dead spots"**: second-by-second analysis of the retention curve to cut every moment of drop-off
- **Internal cliff-hangers**: create micro-suspense between segments
- **Visible payoff**: immediately show what will happen (the prize, the challenge) to build anticipation
- **End screen with 70-80% retention**: the video must maintain high attention until the very last second

## How to Apply to Your Brand

### Reinvesting in Content
Even with limited budgets, the principle holds: dedicate a fixed percentage of content-generated revenue to producing your next piece of content. If a tutorial video generates leads, invest in a better video. Example: HubSpot consistently reinvests in the quality of its educational videos, raising the bar every quarter.

### Thumbnail-First Thinking
Before producing a video, ask yourself: "What's the thumbnail?" If you can't picture an image that generates clicks, the concept needs to be reworked. For a B2B/SaaS brand, this means: large numbers prominently displayed, a human face with emotion, color contrast with the feed.

### The First 3 Seconds
In [your brand]'s videos, open with the most surprising data point or the most provocative question. Never with the logo. Never with "Hi, today we're talking about..." Example: Notion opens its videos with the final result, then explains how to get there.

### Quality Over Quantity
Better 2 exceptional videos per month than 8 mediocre ones. Every video should be designed to be the best possible on that topic on YouTube. Focus resources, time, and creativity.

### Post-Publication Analysis
After every video, analyze the retention curve second by second. Where does it drop? Why? Use that data to improve the next video. This is the continuous improvement cycle that made MrBeast number one.

## Frameworks and Methodologies

### The HIVE Framework (Hook - Intro - Value - End Screen)
A scripting framework for YouTube videos:
- **Hook** (first 10 seconds): capture attention with a problem, a provocative question, or an emotional connection. Show the viewer what they're about to watch
- **Intro** (10-30 seconds): establish credibility and contextualize the topic. Why you? Why now?
- **Value** (body of the video): the actual content, structured in clear and digestible points. Each point should have a mini-hook of its own
- **End Screen** (last 20-30 seconds): clear CTA toward the next video, subscription, or related resource

### The HIVES Framework (extended variant)
Adds the **S for Story** — integrate personal micro-stories into every section to increase engagement and retention.

### Hedgehog Concept
A positioning framework for creators, derived from Jim Collins:
- What are you passionate about?
- What can you be the best at?
- What does the market want?
The intersection of these three areas is your ideal niche.

### Bridge of Transformation
Competitive analysis: study your competitors, identify where they take their audience (point A -> point B) and find a different or better bridge.

### Artery Method
Repurposing strategy: a long YouTube video becomes the arterial content from which you derive clips for Shorts, Reels, TikTok, tweets, newsletters, and LinkedIn posts.

### Pentagram Model
Five monetization pillars for creators: AdSense, sponsorship, digital products, membership/community, services/coaching.

## Hook and Retention Techniques

- **Problem hook**: "90% of people get this wrong..." — creates urgency and curiosity
- **Result hook**: show the final result in the first seconds, then explain the journey
- **Visual pattern interrupts**: angle changes, B-roll, animated graphics every 15-20 seconds
- **List structure with preview**: "5 techniques I used to..." — the number creates expectation, the list maintains structure
- **Personal micro-stories**: brief anecdotes (30-60 seconds) between points to refresh attention
- **Open loop**: preview the next point before closing the current one

## How to Apply to Your Brand

### HIVE Framework for Corporate Videos
Every video from [your brand] should follow the HIVE structure. Hook in the first 10 seconds (surprising data, provocative question), intro that establishes authority, value structured in clear points, end screen with CTA. Example: Canva uses exactly this structure in its YouTube tutorials.

### Part-Time Content Strategy
You don't need a full-time video team to get started. With the right framework, even a founder dedicating 4 hours per week can produce 1 quality video. The Artery Method then lets you derive 5-8 social content pieces from each video.

### Hedgehog Concept for Positioning
Find the intersection of: what your team is passionate about + what your brand does better than anyone + what the market is looking for. That's your content sweet spot. For a B2B SaaS, it could be the intersection of technical expertise, customer pain points, and market trends.

### Adapted Pentagram Monetization
Even if [your brand] doesn't monetize YouTube directly, the 5-pillar model adapts: awareness (views), lead generation (CTA), nurturing (video series), conversion (demo/trial), retention (community). Every video should serve at least one of these goals.

### Systematic Consistency
The secret isn't publishing every day but publishing with predictable regularity. 1 video per week, same day, same quality. The audience learns when to expect you. HubSpot publishes every Tuesday and Thursday — their subscribers know it.

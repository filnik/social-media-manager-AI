# Daniele Doesn't Matter -- YouTube Expert Profile

## Profile
- **Who he is**: Creator, storyteller, and pioneer of the Italian web. Born Daniele Selvitella in Casale Monferrato in 1987, he holds a degree in Design Sciences from IAAD. He opened his YouTube channel in late 2009, becoming one of the first successful Italian YouTubers. His format blends social commentary, sharp irony, and commentary on web phenomena. He has published two books with Mondadori: "Come -- Come diventare famosi stando comodamente seduti in poltrona" (How -- How to Become Famous While Sitting Comfortably in Your Armchair, 2013) and "E Buonanotte -- La storia del ragazzo senza sonno" (And Goodnight -- The Story of the Boy Without Sleep, 2017). He has been collaborating with RDS since 2016 with segments on web and technology. Since 2026, he hosts "100 secondi con Daniele Doesn't Matter" (100 Seconds with Daniele Doesn't Matter) on RDS.
- **YouTube subscribers**: ~1 million
- **Total views**: over 182 million
- **Specialization**: serial storytelling, social commentary, recurring formats, cultural commentary
- **Channel**: Daniele Doesn't Matter

## Core Philosophy
1. **Serialization as loyalty-building**: the episodic format with a fixed schedule (every Friday) creates a habit in the viewer. It's not a channel, it's a show.
2. **Social commentary through irony**: don't preach, make people reflect while laughing. Irony is the tool for addressing serious topics without being heavy-handed.
3. **Interaction as content**: viewers' comments become part of the next video. The audience isn't passive, they're co-authors.
4. **Long-term format consistency**: from 2009 to today, the core format has remained recognizable while evolving. Consistency and recognizability beat constant reinvention.
5. **Web anthropology**: YouTube isn't just entertainment, it's a mirror of society. Commenting on web phenomena means commenting on contemporary culture.

## Frameworks and Methodologies
- **"Cringe + Commentary" format**: a recurring structure -- (1) introduction with a mini everyday story, (2) 4-5 selected web videos/phenomena, (3) ironic commentary with underlying social critique, (4) closing with interaction (question to the audience).
- **Weekly serialization**: one video every Friday, with narrative continuity between episodes. Comments from the previous episode open the next one, creating an engagement loop.
- **Interactive book**: in the book "Come," each chapter interweaves with videos accessible via QR codes, creating a cross-media experience.
- **Narrator-character**: Daniele isn't a neutral host -- he's a character with a recognizable voice, strong opinions, and a clear point of view.
- **Comedic escalation**: structuring the video with a crescendo of absurdity -- the phenomena presented become progressively more extreme, with the commentary growing sharper.
- **Multi-platform with adapted formats**: YouTube (weekly long-form), RDS ("100 seconds"), Instagram, podcast -- each platform has its own format but the same voice.

## Hook and Retention Techniques
- **Opening with an everyday personal story**: every video starts with an anecdote from Daniele's real life -- a mundane situation told with irony that creates connection and introduces the theme.
- **Curated selection of featured content**: the selected videos/phenomena are chosen to maximize the contrast between the absurdity of the content and the lucidity of the commentary.
- **Consistent narrative rhythm**: approximately 10 minutes per episode, with a calibrated pace that leaves no dead time. Each segment has its own comedic payoff.
- **Direct engagement**: "What do you think?", "Reply in the comments", "Send me your videos" -- interaction is a structural part of the format, not an add-on.
- **Closing with a social cliffhanger**: ending with a provocative question or observation that lingers, pushing the viewer to comment and come back next week.

## Memorable Quotes and Concepts
1. "Perche' le persone vogliono diventare fenomeni di YouTube umiliandosi? Questa e' la domanda. E la risposta dice molto sulla nostra societa'." (Why do people want to become YouTube sensations by humiliating themselves? That's the question. And the answer says a lot about our society.)
2. "Non faccio satira, faccio antropologia con la telecamera accesa." (I don't do satire, I do anthropology with the camera on.)
3. "Il web e' il miglior specchio della societa'. Se non ti piace quello che vedi, il problema non e' lo specchio." (The web is the best mirror of society. If you don't like what you see, the problem isn't the mirror.)
4. "Mille video, stesso format, ogni venerdi'. La costanza non e' noia, e' identita'." (A thousand videos, same format, every Friday. Consistency isn't boredom, it's identity.)
5. "I miei spettatori non sono un pubblico, sono co-autori. Senza i loro commenti, il video successivo non esisterebbe." (My viewers aren't an audience, they're co-authors. Without their comments, the next video wouldn't exist.)

## How to Apply to Your Brand
- **Serialization as a loyalty strategy**: [your brand] can create a YouTube series with a fixed schedule (e.g., every Tuesday). A recurring, recognizable format builds habit and loyalty. Example: Notion publishes "How I Use Notion" on a regular cadence, creating an appointment for the community.
- **Industry commentary as a format**: create a segment that comments on the news, trends, and absurdities of your industry with irony and a clear point of view. Works as a "video editorial" that positions the brand as an authoritative voice.
- **Interaction as a content engine**: use the audience's comments, questions, and feedback as content for subsequent videos. The audience feels part of the project and keeps coming back.
- **Recognizable voice and strong point of view**: don't be neutral. Having a declared opinion, a recognizable tone, and a unique perspective sets [your brand] apart from the mass of generic content.
- **Cross-media with adapted formats**: the same topic can be covered in 10 minutes on YouTube, in 100 seconds as a podcast/reel, in a LinkedIn post, and in a newsletter, adapting the format to the platform.

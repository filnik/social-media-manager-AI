## Core Philosophy
1. **Google Liquido** (Liquid Google): Google is no longer a traditional search engine -- it's a liquid ecosystem that redistributes content through AI, Knowledge Graph, YouTube, and other surfaces. Content must serve as a "source" for this ecosystem.
2. **YouTube is part of the Google ecosystem**: whoever understands how Google works understands YouTube. SEO, search intent, and content strategy apply to both.
3. **Share to grow**: knowledge sharing and dissemination are the engine of professional growth and community building. At the core, there's always the need to share ideas.
4. **The era of semantic search**: keywords aren't enough anymore. YouTube and Google reason by entity, context, and intent. Content must answer real questions.
5. **Continuous learning**: the algorithm changes, platforms evolve. Those who don't keep up fall behind. Ongoing education is a must, not an option.

## Frameworks and Methodologies
- **YouTube SEO Framework**: optimization of title, description, tags, chapters, and subtitles to maximize discoverability on both YouTube and Google Search.
- **Content as Source (Google Liquido)**: creating "source" content designed to be reprocessed and redistributed by AI and algorithms across multiple platforms.
- **Intent Mapping for YouTube**: analyzing search intent (informational, navigational, transactional) and creating videos that precisely address each type of query.
- **Chapters and Key Moments**: structuring videos with timestamps and chapters to improve both the user experience and rankings in Google SERPs (video snippets).
- **Cross-platform Distribution**: using YouTube as the central hub of a strategy that includes newsletter (FastLetter), community, live events, and training.
- **Entity-based Video SEO**: optimizing videos not just for keywords but for entities (people, concepts, brands) recognized by Google's Knowledge Graph.

## Hook and Retention Techniques
- **Opening with the news**: in update videos, always lead with the most relevant data point or development. The viewer must immediately understand why this video is urgent.
- **"Information pill" format**: short, dense videos that get straight to the point. Less is more, especially for a professional audience.
- **Historical contextualization**: connecting every development to a broader trend for depth. "Google changed X, and this is what it means for your business."
- **Chapter structure**: each section of the video is self-contained. Viewers can skip to the part they care about, increasing overall watch time.
- **Call to action to the newsletter**: integrating YouTube with the newsletter to create a multi-platform loyalty loop.

## How to Apply to Your Brand
- **YouTube SEO for B2B/SaaS**: [your brand] can optimize every video with titles based on real target audience searches, context-rich descriptions, timestamps, and subtitles. This improves rankings on both YouTube and Google SERPs.
- **Videos as "source content"**: create in-depth videos designed to be cited, reprocessed, and redistributed by AI (Google SGE, ChatGPT). Example: HubSpot creates tutorial videos that appear in Google's AI-generated answers.
- **Intent Mapping for your industry**: map out the questions your target audience searches on YouTube and create specific videos for each intent. "How to choose a tool for X" (informational) vs "Tool X vs Tool Y" (comparative).
- **Chapters and key moments**: structure every video with clear timestamps to appear in Google video snippets and improve user experience.
